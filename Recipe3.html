Here’s a simple step-by-step recipe to make flavorful vegetable biryani:

Ingredients

For Rice:

  • 1.5 cups Basmati rice
  • 6 cups water
  • 2-3 cloves
  • 2 green cardamom
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 tsp salt

For Vegitable Layer:

  • 2 tbsp oil or ghee
  • 1 onion (thinly sliced)
  • 1 tsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 tomatoes (chopped)
  • 1-2 green chilies (slit)
  • 2 mixed vegetables (carrot, peas, potatoes, beans, cauliflower)
  • ½ cup yogurt (whisked)
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • 1 tsp biryani masala
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves (chopped)
  • Fresh mint leaves (chopped)

For Layering:

  • Saffron strands soaked in 2 tbsp warm milk (optional)
  • 2 tbsp fried onions
  • 1 tbsp ghee
  • Fresh coriander and mint leaves

Instructions

1: Cook the Rice

  • Rinse the basmati rice in cold water until the water runs clear.
  • In a large pot, boil 6 cups of water and add the whole spices (cloves, cardamom, bay leaf, cinnamon stick) and salt.
  • Add the rinsed rice to the boiling water and cook until it’s 70-80% cooked (still firm).
  • Drain the rice and set aside.

2: Prepare the Vegetables

  • Heat 2 tbsp oil or ghee in a pan. Add the sliced onions and sauté until golden brown.
  • Add the ginger-garlic paste and sauté until the raw smell goes away.
  • Add chopped tomatoes and green chilies, cooking until the tomatoes soften.
  • Add the mixed vegetables and stir well.
  • Add the yogurt, red chili powder, turmeric powder, garam masala, biryani masala, and salt. Mix everything well.
  • Cover and cook the vegetables on low heat until they are tender but not mushy. Stir occasionally.
  • Once the vegetables are cooked, sprinkle chopped coriander and mint leaves.

3: Layering the Biryani

  • In a large heavy-bottomed pot or handi, spread a layer of cooked vegetables at the bottom.
  • Add a layer of partially cooked rice over the vegetables.
  • Sprinkle some saffron milk (optional), fried onions, chopped coriander, and mint leaves on top.
  • Repeat the layering process, ending with a final layer of rice and toppings of saffron milk, fried onions, and herbs.

4: Dum Cooking (Steaming)

  • Drizzle ghee over the top layer.
  • Cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid (or seal the edges with dough) to trap the steam.
  • Place the pot on low heat and cook for 15-20 minutes (this is called "dum").
  • Turn off the heat and let it rest for another 10 minutes.

5: Serve

  • Gently fluff up the biryani layers with a fork, being careful not to break the rice grains.
  • Serve the veg biryani hot with raita, papad, or salad.

Enjoy your delicious homemade vegetable biryani!
